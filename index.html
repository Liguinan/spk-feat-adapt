Towards Zero-Shot Speaker-Adaptive Audio-Visual Multichannel Speech Separation and Recognition

Authors: xxx

Abstract: This paper proposes zero-shot, enrolment-free speaker adaptation approaches for multichannel speech separation and recognition systems. Speaker features are on-the-fly learned from overlapped mixture speech input using: a) cross-utterance speaker features smoothing; and b) joint optimization of speaker features and speech separation systems. Experiments are conducted on the mixture overlapped speech data constructed using the LRS3-TED and replayed LRS2 datasets. Our zero-shot speaker adaptation approaches consistently outperform the speaker-independent (SI) baselines using mask-based MVDR speech separation and WavLM features empowered Conformer ASR on speech enhancement metrics including SISNR, PESQ and STOI, with or without incorporating video modality. Statistically significant ASR word error rate (WER) reductions of up to 2.2%, 2.8% and 0.8% absolute (15.7%, 28.3% and 9.5% relative) are obtained on the LRS3 dev, test and LRS2 eval datasets, respectively. Empirical analyses suggest our zero-shot approaches effectively break the link between speaker-level data quantity and performance gains from adaptation. T-SNE visualization shows that our approaches produce more homogenous and consistent speaker features than standard x-vector and ECAPA-TDNN. Further analyses reveal that these improvements are strongly correlated with the increase of inter-speaker discrimination measured using cosine similarity. Speaker adaptation inference time speedup ratios up to 27.3 times is achieved over offline, enrolment-based adaptation.

  • (a) Audio-visual multichannel speech separation backbone implemented using TCNs and mask-based MVDR beamforming.
  • (b) Enrolment-based speaker adaptation, which uses offline recording of sufficient quantities of speaker-level clean speech data.
  • (c) Enrolment-free speaker adaptation, which directly learns homogenous speaker features on the fly from mixed speech input.

III. Experimental Setup and Results

A. Experimental setup

  • A 15-channel symmetric linear array with non-even inter-channel spacing is leveraged to simulate the multichannel mixture speech using the LRS3-TED dataset [6] with 10354, 3122, 3136 and 5284 utterances respectively for training (111.47 hours, 4886 speakers), validation (3.15 hours, 157 speakers), dev (3.14 hours, 158 speakers) and test (3.36 hours, 412 speakers).
  • A replayed eval dataset containing 1200 (0.5 hours, 340 speakers) utterances is recorded by replaying two loudspeakers simultaneously to generate a 15-channel mixture speech in a meeting room [1], based on the Oxford LRS2 test set. The geometric specification of the microphone array used during recording is the same as that used in the simulation.
  • Espnet style Conformer AED ASR model [1] contains 12 encoder and 6 decoder layers, Whisper-medium[7] and WavLM-large[8] are used.
